I love exoplanets! I've wanted to study them since I was in high school (back in the days when there weren't even 500 confirmed exoplanets). I've tailored my academics towards this goal. I made a custom degree at RIT for Experimental Astrophysics, where I take a combination of imaging science and physics courses.

I'm a little older than most undergrads (24), and I've spent 3 years working alongside school. I've developed processing pipelines and a NIR spectrometer for an asteroid mining company, a machine learning framework for a Rochester imaging company, and spent a year developing pipelines and instruments for a lab at the University of Rochester. I also won a special innovation award at a design competition, and built an imaging/inspection robot for SpaceX. (see my resume for details).

I'm a specialist in imaging and software design, and I have practical experience in instrumentation and hardware. I want to put it to use discovering life outside our solar system!

I feel very strongly that good software accelerates research, while bad software can hold it back. To this end, I created ImagePypelines - an open-source python library built for scientists who might struggle with coding. The goal is to build a tool that will let scientists develop robust code with only a script. We take care of the coding nuance like error checking, modularity, parallelism, etc.

In essence, ImagePypelines lets scientists create powerful processing pipelines at a high level without worrying about the nitty-gritty details of coding. We want to make coding more accessible for all scientists, and I hope this will help accelerate research moving forward.

Plugins for Astronomy and Imaging Science are in the works. Our first beta will be released Aug. 2020!

I also got to work on NIR spectrometer prototypes in the cleanroom and learned an enormous amount about planetary and small body sciences! (and the practical challenges of studying them).

This company's mission was very near and dear to my heart - I want to contribute to the science that will eventually get us off this planet.
